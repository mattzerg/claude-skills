How it fits

The constellation in motion.

Watch how a single piece of work moves across the stack — from meeting to ticket to chat to calendar to email — without humans copy-pasting between five apps and without agents needing five OAuth flows.

  1. 01

    ZergMeeting

    A meeting wraps

    ZergMeeting captures the transcript and surfaces action items as the meeting ends. Humans skim; agents extract.

  2. 02

    Zergboard

    Actions land as cards

    The right items become cards on Zergboard — some filed by humans, others by an agent reading the meeting output or a webhook.

  3. 03

    ZergChat

    The team gets pinged

    A relevant channel sees the new ticket. An agent posts context, links the related work, and threads with whoever is on point.

  4. 04

    ZergCal

    Time gets blocked

    When work needs human attention, an agent finds the right window in ZergCal — across humans and other agents — and books it.

  5. 05

    ZergMail

    The right people are looped in

    Stakeholders outside the workspace get a programmatic email summary with the ticket, the thread, and the calendar invite — all from one workflow.

Why ZergStack

Small tools that compose into a real platform.

The big workspace suites are bloated and expensive. The single-purpose tools do not talk to each other. ZergStack is the third option — a constellation of small tools that share workspace identity, expose real APIs, and run cheaply on infrastructure you control.

Built for agents from day one

API-first, not API-bolted-on.

Every ZergStack product exposes a stable, documented API before it gets a UI. Agent automation is a first-class workflow, not a partner integration on a roadmap.

No enterprise bloat

Tools that fit on one screen.

ZergStack tools are deliberately small. Zergboard does not need 47 plugins. ZergCal does not need a CRM hiding inside it. Each product owns one job and gets out of the way.

Workspaces talk to each other

One identity. Linked state.

Tickets in Zergboard, threads in ZergChat, events in ZergCal, captures in ZergMeeting, and emails in ZergMail share workspace identity. Cross-product references resolve. The constellation behaves like a platform without the platform tax.

Self-host or hosted

You decide where it runs.

Each ZergStack product runs as a small, self-contained service. Use the hosted path or run it inside your own VPC. No data lock-in, no proprietary protocols.
