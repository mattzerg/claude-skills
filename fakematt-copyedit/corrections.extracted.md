## 2026-05-06 — Idan, AdaExplore explainer ("What AI Agents Get Wrong About Failure")

### Keep em-dash count to ≤ 3 in pieces of ≤ 800 words; convert conversational asides to periods

**Why:** Pulp/social register reads best with periods; em-dashes should be load-bearing, not decorative filler
**When:** Short pulp-register blog posts (≤ 800 words) distributed on social or LinkedIn

**Before:** `…trying again — often with the same broken approach…`
**After:** `…trying again. Often with the same broken approach…`

### Give sharp single-sentence central-frame claims their own paragraph

**Why:** White space sells punchlines; burying them mid-paragraph dilutes their impact
**When:** Pulp-register blog posts where one sentence carries the core argument

**Before:** `The retry loop is the default, and the default is wrong. We notice this at Zerg, so a new paper…`
**After:** `The retry loop is the default, and the default is wrong.

We notice this at Zerg…`

### Cap figure captions at ≤ 20 words in pulp; use numeric shorthand when data supports; cut any caption sentence that restates the body

**Why:** Pulp social captions are billboards, not paragraphs; duplication wastes reader attention
**When:** Figures and images in short pulp or social blog posts

**Before:** `Figure 1 shows the dramatic reduction from 1,178 tokens to 174 and then 78, while single-pass correctness improved from 22% to 54%.`
**After:** `1,178 → 174 → 78. Single-pass correctness: 22% → 54%.`

### Name the baseline explicitly whenever citing a speedup or improvement ratio

**Why:** Vague baselines undermine credibility with sharp readers and obscure what was actually measured
**When:** Any quantitative claim in blog posts, explainers, or launch copy using 'over baseline' / 'vs baseline' / 'than baseline'

**Before:** `3.12× speedup over baseline within 100 steps.`
**After:** `3.12× over PyTorch eager within 100 steps.`

### Use exactly one call to action in the closer; choose the more confident and specific one

**Why:** Two CTAs split focus and dilute the closer's punch; filler CTAs weaken the specific one
**When:** Pulp blog post closers and email sign-offs

**Before:** `we'd love to compare notes — come argue with us about what should and shouldn't transfer.`
**After:** `Come argue with us about what should and shouldn't transfer.`

### Replace 'competitive with X' / 'comparable to X' / 'in line with X' with a verb-forward phrase grounded in the actual data

**Why:** Pulp register wants verbs; nominal comparisons are weak and imprecise
**When:** Performance claims or comparisons in blog posts and launch copy

**Before:** `competitive with vendor-optimized code`
**After:** `as fast as vendor-optimized code`

### Move gap-admission and strategic-disclosure lines to a separate 'Strategic disclosure' section rather than flagging them as writing-quality findings

**Why:** These are CEO/author judgment calls, not copyedit issues; mixing them confuses the review and the responsibility
**When:** Copy reviews of blog posts or launch copy that include lines admitting a product gap or unfinished capability

**Before:** `[Flagging 'we don't have ours yet' as a soft writing finding under prose quality]`
**After:** `[Move to 'Strategic disclosure' section with note: author/CEO should decide whether to keep this admission]`

### When a draft title uses a 'What X Gets Wrong About Y' or 'Why X Is Failing Y' curiosity-gap shape, find the flat declarative version of the same claim in the body or share copy and recommend it as the primary title

**Why:** Flat declarative headlines are sharper and more pulp; curiosity-gap shapes are overused and signal dated content
**When:** Headline review for pulp-register blog posts, especially those with existing LinkedIn share copy

**Before:** `What AI Agents Get Wrong About Failure`
**After:** `Most AI Agents Have No Memory`
