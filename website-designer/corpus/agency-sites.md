# Agency / consultancy site corpus — Vang Advisory references

## Premium agency sites Matt rates 8+

### pentagram.com
- Case-study-led, not service-list led
- One huge work image per project page
- Restrained but authoritative
- **Take**: Work first. Services emerge from the work, not stated abstractly.

### thoughtbot.com
- Services structure done well — but with specificity (named methodologies, real client names inline)
- Strong typographic system, not Inter+Fraunces
- **Take**: When you must list services, do it with specificity (named processes, real outcomes), not "we help with growth."

### workandco.com
- Black-tie agency feel — minimal but premium
- Single-font system
- Photography forward
- **Take**: Confidence through restraint.

### high-resolution.studio
- Bold typography, not stock-template
- Distinctive grid system
- **Take**: Bold but not templated.

### pineapple.studio (boutique consultancy)
- Specific clients named in body text
- Personality in voice
- **Take**: Boutique consultancies should feel boutique — not enterprise.

### zone-7.studio
- Services with character
- Idiosyncratic navigation
- **Take**: Even small studios can have non-stencil structure.

## What these share

- Work / case studies dominate, not abstract service descriptions
- Typography has identity (custom or considered pairing)
- Specific clients named, not "60+ clients"
- One hero image or text moment, not 6 competing hero elements
- No animated gradient blobs
- No 4-stat strip
- No "01 / 02 / 03" service numbering
- No 3 testimonial cards in a row

## Specifically wrong for Vang Advisory

- "Five things, well." — generic
- "01 Growth + GTM / 02 Product analytics / 03 Accelerator design / 04 Investment due diligence / 05 Fractional growth" — exactly the stencil pentagram avoids
- Numbered service cards
- Animated teal blob art
- 4-stat strip ("60+ / 6 / $100M+ / 7-fig")

## What Vang Advisory should look like

The brand promise is "growth, GTM, program design — for early-stage companies and the people who fund them. 60+ clients across 6 continents."

Possible distinctive structures:
- A list of named engagements as the hero (like a CV's experience section but designed) — "Algorand: pre-launch positioning. Codility: $100M ARR roadmap. RELAYTO: pricing redesign." Three or four real engagements as the headline.
- A timeline view from 2017 → today with named moments
- An asymmetric hero with one specific quote from a client (not 3 cards — one quote, big)
- Type-only first screen (no images, no blob, just a beautiful set typographic statement)

Distinctiveness budget:
- D1: real engagement names + specifics in hero (not generic "we help")
- D2: type-driven design, no blob, no decorative shape
- D3: footer as 1-line OR maximalist — not corporate 4-col
