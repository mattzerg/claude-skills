# VC fund site corpus — Vang Capital references

## Premium fund sites Matt rates 8+

### benchmark.com
- Restrained, minimal, partner-driven
- Plain typography (often custom)
- No portfolio logo grid as the hero
- **Take**: Restraint signals confidence. The fund doesn't need to prove itself with logos.

### founders.fund
- Bold typography, narrative hero
- Manifesto-style content
- **Take**: Funds with strong POV lead with the POV, not the portfolio.

### thrive.com
- Image-led, polished, but not template
- Few sections, dense content per section
- **Take**: When using imagery, use it big and intentional.

### a16z.com
- Content-led not portfolio-led — they publish, so they show that
- **Take**: Differentiate by what you do beyond invest (publish, build, host events)

### spark.capital
- Restraint over pomp
- Type-forward
- **Take**: Less is more for a fund.

### generalcatalyst.com
- Premium without busy
- Strong typography
- **Take**: One thing per screen.

### usv.com (Union Square Ventures)
- Blog-first, fund-second
- Very restrained
- **Take**: The blog is the fund's identity.

## What these share

- Most do NOT lead with portfolio logos
- Strong typography (often custom)
- One narrative hero, not 6 competing elements
- No animated gradient blobs
- No 4-stat strip
- Restrained color (often near-monochrome, or one strong accent)

## Specifically wrong for Vang Capital

- Animated navy blob art in hero
- "30+ / 50% / $50M+ / 8" stat strip
- "Investment thesis / Strategic value-add" 2-card layout
- Generic logo grid as primary content

## What Vang Capital should look like

Brand promise: "Pre-seed bets on data-centric companies. Founded 2019. 30+ investments."

Possible distinctive structures:
- A single thesis statement at huge scale, possibly with custom set type. Below it: a single deep portfolio callout (one company, real story).
- Or: list-as-design — every investment as a typographic line, well-set, like a list of poems.
- Or: a single image of a portfolio company's product/team, full-bleed, with thesis as overlay.

Distinctiveness budget:
- D1: thesis statement at huge scale, type-only hero (no blob, no buttons)
- D2: list-of-investments as the primary content, set typographically (not a logo grid)
- D3: 1-line footer OR a footnote-style author/byline at the bottom (like a research paper)
