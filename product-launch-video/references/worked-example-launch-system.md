Use this as a quality model, not as reusable copy.

## Brief

- Product: Atlas Clips
- Launch moment: public beta
- Audience: B2B founders and marketing leads
- Promise: turn one recorded customer call into a week of credible short-form marketing clips
- Proof available: upload recording, transcript highlights, auto-generated clip candidates, caption editor, vertical/export variants
- Channels: homepage, LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Product Hunt, sales email
- CTA: book a demo
- Architecture: one 90s anchor video plus five 20-30s clips

## Creative Direction

- Territory: Before/After Workflow
- Thesis: For lean B2B teams, Atlas Clips turns long customer conversations into publishable proof assets without waiting on a video editor.
- Core mechanic: upload one call, select moments, generate captioned clips, export channel-ready versions.
- Viewer before: knows recorded calls contain good stories but lacks time to mine and edit them.
- Viewer after: believes one existing conversation can become several credible launch and sales assets.

## Anchor Video Arc

| Time | Beat | Script / Audio | Visual Proof | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0-5s | Hook | "Your best launch clips are probably buried in calls you already recorded." | Grid of long call recordings, then one quote highlighted in transcript. | First frame: transcript highlight plus generated vertical clip. |
| 5-15s | Old way | "Teams spend hours scrubbing webinars, demos, and customer calls for one usable moment." | Timeline scrubbing, messy notes, folder of recordings. | Make the pain concrete, not melodramatic. |
| 15-25s | Reveal | "Atlas Clips finds the moments, drafts the cut, and formats it for launch channels." | Product name, upload recording, transcript appears. | Name product plainly. |
| 25-55s | Core proof | "Upload a call. Pick the moments that sound like proof. Turn them into captioned clips." | Upload > transcript highlights > clip candidates > caption editor > preview. | This is the main proof stretch; avoid fast feature tour. |
| 55-72s | Expansion | "Make one version for LinkedIn, one for X, one for the sales follow-up." | Aspect-ratio/export selector with 16:9, 9:16, 1:1 previews. | Show output variety. |
| 72-84s | Trust | "Keep the source, transcript, and quote context attached so the story stays accurate." | Source quote panel and clip metadata. | Addresses credibility/accuracy concern. |
| 84-90s | CTA | "Turn the calls you already have into launch assets. Book a demo." | End card: Atlas Clips, one-line promise, URL/CTA. | Hold long enough to read. |

## Shared Capture Plan

| Founder line | Talking head | 20-30s clean take | Capture with room tone and b-roll gaps. |
| End cards | Motion/graphic | Master plus channel CTAs | Need 16:9, 9:16, 1:1. |

| Product Hunt | 20s | Product Hunt | First frame says what it does; product name visible throughout. |
| Founder | 25s | LinkedIn, launch post | Human hook; product proof appears by 5s. |
| Sales | 30s | Sales email | Buyer pain and customer quote context. |
| Objection | 25s | Retargeting, sales follow-up | Source/context proof and accuracy framing. |

## Measurement Plan

- Anchor: homepage engagement, CTA click, demo conversion, launch blog completion.
- LinkedIn/X clips: 3-second hold, completion, profile/site clicks, comments from target buyers.
- Product Hunt clip: click-through, upvotes, product-page conversion.
- Sales clip: email thumbnail clicks, reply rate, booked meetings.
- Decision rule: if short clips earn attention but not clicks, test CTA and first-frame product clarity; if anchor earns engagement but low conversion, tighten CTA placement and landing-page continuity.

## Approval Risks

- Customer quote/source context must be permission-safe.
- Any time-saving claim needs a real benchmark or must be phrased as a workflow example.
- Product UI must reflect beta availability.
- Captions and exports need accessibility review.
