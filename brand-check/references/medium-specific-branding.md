# Medium-Specific Branding

## Swag

- Use object anatomy first. Place logos on zones that exist in real production: neck labels, sleeve hits, hem tags, back yokes, mug handle side, sticker backing card, notebook spine, lanyard repeat.
- Do not put full logos into every available visual panel. One strong logo placement per item is usually enough.
- For campaign-heavy items, put the campaign graphic on the hero zone and the full logo in a quieter signature zone.

## Slides And PDFs

- Put wordmark in a stable header/footer or title-card zone, not every dense content card.
- Avoid repeating logos on the same page unless one is part of a product screenshot.
- Logo size should feel like provenance, not a headline, unless it is a title card.

## Landing Pages And Screenshots

- Preserve site navigation/header logo conventions.
- Do not add extra logos to screenshots unless clearly annotated.
- Brand color should guide emphasis, not flood every CTA/card.

## Social And Launch Graphics

- One brand signature zone per asset: top-left, bottom-left, or centered endcap.
- If the headline is the hero, keep logo small.
- If logo is the hero, reduce headline and supporting marks.

## Vendor Files

- Source logo paths should be traceable.
- Document dimensions, colors, and intended print method.
- Export review previews and production masters separately.
