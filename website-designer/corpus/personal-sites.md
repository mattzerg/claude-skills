# Personal site corpus — sites Matt rates 8+/10

Annotated reference for what makes each work. Not all sites are appropriate as direct templates — they're starting points to study before designing.

## Designer/operator personal sites

### frankchimero.com
- One absurdly large section per screen, massive whitespace
- Custom typography (designer's own choice — not Inter+Fraunces)
- Single hero photo, often unconventional crop
- Idiosyncratic structure — not "Hero / Currently / Selected / CTA"
- No footer corporate-style
- **Take**: hierarchy through size + restraint. Less is more, but with strong typographic personality.

### jasonsantamaria.com
- Single-font system, set with care
- Small but distinctive details (kerning, weight contrast)
- Minimal sections, high text density per section
- **Take**: type as identity. The whole site is one careful typographic system.

### robinrendle.com
- Bold pull quotes that break the column grid
- Personality in voice + structure
- Long-form writing as a main surface (not separate "blog")
- **Take**: voice is structural. The site enacts the writer's tone.

### danluu.com
- Extreme density — anti-pattern in some contexts but works for him because the content is dense
- No images, no design polish
- **Take**: when you have the content, the design can recede entirely

### pieter.com (Pieter Levels)
- Extreme personality, specific projects with specific revenue numbers
- Crude typography on purpose
- The site IS the brand
- **Take**: idiosyncrasy + specificity beats polish. "$X MRR from project Y" > "I help companies grow."

### jeremykeith.com / adactio.com
- Dense personal blog feel
- Custom typography
- Clear voice
- **Take**: opinionated structure organized around the content the person actually produces

### harshjv.com
- Premium personal feel without templates
- Custom navigation pattern
- **Take**: modern but not Webflow-templated

### alvaromontoro.com
- Distinctive hero treatment (often code-driven art)
- **Take**: the medium IS the content for makers who code

## What these all share

- Custom font system (not Inter+Fraunces by default)
- Idiosyncratic structure (not Hero/Currently/Selected/CTA)
- One thing dominates per screen
- Voice is structural, not just paragraph-level
- Real photography or restrained imagery
- 3+ distinctive details visible to a casual visitor

## Anti-corpus (what NOT to look like)

- v0.dev / Lovable.dev default personal site outputs
- ThemeForest "creative agency 2024" templates
- Webflow Marketplace "Personal Portfolio Pro" templates
- Any of these tells: animated gradient blobs, 4-stat strip, hover-up cards, soft-tint alt sections, 3 testimonial cards, 4-col footer
