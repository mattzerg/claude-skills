---
id: porter-5f
name: Porter's Five Forces
forces: ["Rivalry among existing competitors", "Threat of new entrants", "Bargaining power of suppliers", "Bargaining power of buyers", "Threat of substitute products"]
when_to_use:
  - Assessing structural attractiveness of an industry the company is considering entering or doubling down on
  - Understanding why margins are pressured in a sector
  - Making an entry / exit / scaling decision at the category level
when_not_to_use:
  - Single-product / single-feature decisions (too zoomed out — use cohort analysis or competitive matrix)
  - High-growth markets where the structure is still forming (forces are unstable; use Three Horizons instead)
  - Network-effect markets where winner-take-most dominates structural forces
anti_patterns:
  - Treating all five forces as equal weight
  - Skipping substitutes (the single force most often missed)
  - No quantitative anchor — "high / medium / low" with no numbers
chart_recipe: heatmap
---

Each force scored 1–5 with one sentence of evidence + one named number per force.
