# Creative Strategy

Use this when a launch-video request needs concept development, stronger positioning, or multiple creative routes before scripting.

## Strategy Inputs

- Audience: who needs to care now
- Moment: why this launch matters now
- Tension: what current behavior is broken, slow, risky, expensive, boring, or newly possible
- Promise: the change the product makes believable
- Proof: what can be shown, not just claimed
- Brand stance: practical, provocative, premium, technical, playful, founder-led, category-defining, or customer-led
- Distribution reality: where the video must win

## Concept Territories

Offer 2-4 territories when the brief is early. Keep each territory short:

| Territory | Core Idea | Opening Image | Proof Mechanic | Tone | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## Useful Territory Types

- **Before/After Workflow**: Show the old way collapsing into the new way.
- **Impossible Demo**: Lead with the product doing the hardest-to-believe thing.
- **New Category Claim**: Name the old category limitation and show the new behavior.
- **Founder Thesis**: Human explanation of why the team built it, cut with product proof.
- **Customer Proof**: Use a customer's constraint and outcome as the spine.
- **Speedrun**: Compress a formerly long workflow into a legible short sequence.
- **Launch Signal**: Make the moment feel public, useful, and worth sharing.

## Selection Rules

- Prefer the territory with the strongest visible proof, not the cleverest line.
- Avoid a concept that requires footage, claims, or customer permission the team does not have.
- If the product is visually quiet, make the transformation visible through before/after states, data movement, human reaction, or time compression.
- If the product is complex, use a narrow workflow rather than a category manifesto.
