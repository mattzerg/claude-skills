# Script Patterns

> **For measured numbers** (VO cadence, words-per-beat budgets, hook word-counts, beat durations per format), use `script-techniques.md` — it supersedes the prose below wherever a measured value exists. These patterns give the *shape*; the techniques catalog gives the *numbers*.

## Product Launch

Use this when a product, feature, company milestone, waitlist, funding, version, or public availability moment is the news.

1. Hook: name the shift, pain, or surprising capability.
2. Context: show why existing behavior is broken or slow.
3. Reveal: name the product or feature plainly.
4. Proof: show the product doing the thing, with concrete inputs and outputs.
5. Expansion: show one or two adjacent use cases, not a full feature tour.
6. Trust: add customer, benchmark, founder, technical, or design proof when available.
7. CTA: tell the viewer exactly what to do next.

Avoid starting with "we're excited" or a company-history recap unless the audience already cares.

## Demo Video

Lead with outcome, then show the fewest steps needed to believe it. Use "watch what changes" beats instead of narrating every click.

## Paid / Performance Ad

Open with a specific viewer problem or measurable promise. Use fast proof, short lines, and one CTA. Write several hooks because the opening carries most of the risk.

## Founder-Led Announcement

Use a direct human premise: what changed, why it matters now, what the team learned, and what the viewer can do. Keep product claims visible with supporting B-roll.

## Case Study / Customer Story

Build from before state, constraint, intervention, after state, and quoted proof. Do not invent metrics; mark missing proof as `[needs source]`.

## Brand Film

Use a thesis-driven structure: belief, tension, evidence, invitation. Keep abstraction tied to visual proof.

## Social Short

Make the first frame legible as a paused thumbnail. One mechanic or idea per video. Keep the punchline or transformation early enough that the viewer does not need context from a caption.
