# Clip Slate Generator

Use this to choose a set of 20-30 second modular launch clips around one 75-100 second anchor video.

## Principle

Each short clip needs a distinct job. Do not create five versions of the same feature demo unless the distribution plan specifically calls for hook testing.

## Selection Workflow

1. List the launch goals: awareness, education, conversion, sales enablement, community launch, founder narrative, objection handling.
2. List priority audiences or segments.
3. List available proof moments: product flow, before/after, customer quote, metric, founder POV, technical detail, integration, benchmark.
4. Pick 3-8 clips that cover different jobs across the launch campaign.
5. For each clip, write its own hook and first frame. Reusing footage is fine; reusing the same opening logic is usually weak.
6. Mark whether the clip is sourced from the anchor, needs extra capture, or needs a separate voiceover/talking-head take.

## Default Slate

For a broad B2B software launch, start with 5 clips:

| Clip | Job | Best Channel | Why It Exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pain-to-proof | Show the old workflow collapsing into the new one | LinkedIn, X, paid | Makes the core promise immediately legible. |
| Core product proof | Show the hardest-to-believe product action | Homepage, Product Hunt, X | Makes the launch credible. |
| Use-case segment | Speak to one buyer or workflow | LinkedIn, sales email | Lets distribution target a specific audience. |
| Objection answer | Address the biggest trust blocker | Sales, retargeting, founder post | Reduces hesitation after initial interest. |
| Founder/context | Explain why now and why this team | LinkedIn, launch post | Adds human conviction and narrative. |

## Clip Archetypes

### Pain-To-Proof Clip

- Runtime: 20-30s
- Hook: "This used to take [painful amount of time/steps]."
- Visual: before workflow, then product transformation.
- CTA: try, book, or see full launch.

### Core Proof Clip

- Runtime: 15-30s
- Hook: show the output first or the ambitious input first.
- Visual: one uninterrupted product path from input to result.
- CTA: product name plus action.

### Use-Case Clip

- Runtime: 20-30s
- Hook: name the audience or workflow.
- Visual: realistic data and task for that segment.
- CTA: segment-specific outcome.

### Objection Clip

- Runtime: 20-30s
- Hook: state the likely hesitation directly.
- Visual: proof of setup, quality, security, control, accuracy, cost, or integration.
- CTA: learn more, book, or watch demo.

### Founder Clip

- Runtime: 20-30s
- Hook: direct human claim.
- Visual: founder line plus product proof inserts.
- CTA: launch post, waitlist, or demo.

### Customer / Proof Clip

- Runtime: 20-30s
- Hook: quote, metric, or before/after.
- Visual: customer context plus product result.
- CTA: read story, book, or try.

### Product Hunt / Community Clip

- Runtime: 15-25s
- Hook: "It does X" in the first frame.
- Visual: fast product proof, product name, URL.
- CTA: upvote, try, or share.

## Slate Quality Check

- Does every clip have a unique reason to exist?
- Does every clip show proof, not just claim value?
- Are at least two clips usable without the anchor video?
- Are priority channels covered with native hooks?
- Is there a mix of product-led and human/trust-led material when the launch needs credibility?
- Are extra capture needs listed before production starts?
