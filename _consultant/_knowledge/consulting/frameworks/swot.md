---
id: swot
name: SWOT
quadrants: ["Strengths (internal +)", "Weaknesses (internal -)", "Opportunities (external +)", "Threats (external -)"]
when_to_use:
  - Quick situational snapshot at the start of strategic planning
  - Onboarding new leader to a business's current position
  - Before deeper frameworks (often paired with Porter or value-chain)
when_not_to_use:
  - As the FINAL strategic output (it's a starting point, not an answer)
  - When the question is "what should we do?" — SWOT lists, doesn't decide
  - When items would split internal/external ambiguously (e.g., partnerships)
anti_patterns:
  - Generic adjectives ("strong brand", "market headwinds") without named numbers
  - Equal-length lists in all four quadrants (forced symmetry — usually 2 quadrants dominate)
  - SWOT presented as the recommendation (it's the situation; the recommendation comes from acting on the cross-pairs)
chart_recipe: null
---

Real value is in the cross-pairs: S×O (leverage), W×O (improve), S×T (defend), W×T (avoid). The 4 lists alone are weak.
